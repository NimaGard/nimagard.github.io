Hello World: Why I'm Starting This Blog

Robot Learning Manufacturing

After spending over six years building AI systems that teach robots to perceive, reason about, and manipulate the physical world, I've decided it's time to share what I've learned along the way.

Why now?

The field of robot learning is at an inflection point. Foundation models are finally making it possible to build general-purpose robotic intelligence, and manufacturing — one of the most complex domains for robotics — is where I believe the next breakthrough will happen.

At Path Robotics, we've been tackling this head-on. From building perception systems that understand weld joints in 3D, to developing world models that predict how materials behave, every problem in this space is uniquely challenging and deeply rewarding.

What to expect

This blog will cover a range of topics at the intersection of AI and the physical world:

  • Robot learning — policy learning, world models, sim-to-real transfer
  • Manufacturing AI — the unique challenges of industrial environments
  • Building AI teams — lessons from scaling research to production
  • Technical deep-dives — on methods, architectures, and systems

I'll try to keep things practical and grounded in real experience. No hype, no hand-waving — just honest takes on what works, what doesn't, and why.

Let's connect

If you're working on similar problems, or just curious about the intersection of AI and manufacturing, I'd love to hear from you. Reach out on X/Twitter or LinkedIn.
